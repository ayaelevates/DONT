How to play

The Rules

Each turn

Draw. Choose who answers. Ask.

Active player draws a card. They choose anyone at the table to answer it — or the card can direct the question at themselves. The chosen person gives their answer. No partial answers.

The vote

Everyone votes simultaneously — thumbs up or down

After the answer, all other players show BELIEVE or DOUBT at the same time. Majority BELIEVE = answerer keeps the card and its points. Tie = no one scores. Majority DOUBT = card is discarded, no one scores.

Skipping costs you

Draw and decide — no card hoarding

If you draw a card and refuse to ask it, you lose 1 point (can go negative). You must draw and ask the next card. You cannot skip twice in a row.

Depth check

Surface answers on deep cards get called out

Two consecutive shallow answers on 5 or 10-point cards? Anyone can call a DEPTH CHECK. Group votes. If majority agree — that player loses 3 points and sits out one full round.

Every 2 rounds

Last place takes a consequence

After every 2 complete rounds, the player with the fewest points must complete a Wild card challenge chosen by the player with the most points. No refusing.

Tracking points

Your stack IS your score

Won cards go in your personal pile face-up. At checkpoints and game end, flip your stack and add up the point values printed on each card. No paper, no app. Your pile tells the story.

Minimum players

3 minimum — 4 to 7 is the sweet spot

You need at least 3 for a meaningful vote. 4–7 keeps the energy tight. At 8, rounds get long. Game ends when the deck runs out or the group calls it. Most points wins — but that's not really what this is about.

Wild cards

Relief comes when you least expect it

Wild Relief Cards land randomly in the deck. When drawn, the active player must complete the prompt — no skipping. These break tension and remind everyone why you're here.
